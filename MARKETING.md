# Everyday Studio — Marketing & Launch Playbook

A focused, low-budget plan for taking Everyday Studio from "first app coming soon" to a known indie studio with a real audience.

- **Brand:** Everyday Studio
- **Domain:** `everydaystudio.com`
- **Tagline:** *Useful apps for daily life.*
- **Contact:** `hello@everydaystudio.com`
- **First app:** Cheap Eats (coming soon)

---

## 0. Reality check — where you actually are

Be honest with yourself before spending a single euro on marketing:

- ✅ You have a brand, identity, and website.
- ✅ You have one app close to ready: **Cheap Eats**.
- ⚠️ The other 4 app ideas are concepts, not products. They have **no names, no UI, no users**.
- ⚠️ You have **no audience yet**.

That means the next 90 days are about **ONE thing**: launching Cheap Eats well and building an audience that will be there for app #2.

Don't try to market 5 apps. Market the studio and Cheap Eats.

---

## 1. Brand foundation (lock this once, reuse forever)

### Positioning
> **Everyday Studio is a small, independent studio building useful apps for the moments your day is actually made of. Made by one person, for real people.**

### Tagline options (pick one, use everywhere)
- Primary: **"Useful apps for daily life."**
- Alt 1: *"Apps for the everyday."*
- Alt 2: *"Small apps. Daily use."*
- Alt 3: *"Quietly useful, every day."*

### Voice & tone
- Warm, human, plain English. No corporate-speak.
- Short sentences. Confident, not loud.
- Talk to a single real moment ("you're standing in front of the fridge…"), not abstract benefits.
- Lean into being **one person making this**. That's a feature, not a weakness.

### Visual identity
- **Logo:** amber circle with a dark inner ring.
- **Wordmark:** "Everyday *Studio*" — "Studio" italic, amber, lighter weight.
- **Primary color:** `#f59e0b` (warm amber).
- **Ink:** `#0f172a` (near-black).
- **Background:** `#fbf8f3` (warm off-white).
- **Type:** Inter (UI) + Fraunces (headings).

### App naming convention
Every app you ship in stores is shown as **"<App Name> · by Everyday Studio"**.

- First app: **Cheap Eats · by Everyday Studio**

## 3. Pre-launch: build a tiny audience BEFORE Cheap Eats ships

This is the most important phase. **Don't skip it.** Launching to zero people is the #1 indie mistake.

### Goal: 200 newsletter subscribers + 100 X/Reddit followers before launch day.

### How (4 weeks):

**Week 1 — Make the studio real**
- Publish the website at `everydaystudio.com`.
- Post a "studio launch" thread on X/Twitter telling the story: who you are, why you're building Everyday Studio, what's coming. End with the newsletter link.
- Post the same story (rewritten in long-form) on Indie Hackers, Reddit (r/SideProject), and LinkedIn.

**Week 2 — Start "building in public"**
- Post 3x a week on X/Twitter and 1x a week on Indie Hackers:
  - Day 1: A screenshot or sketch from Cheap Eats.
  - Day 2: A short story about a real cheap-eats moment ("I paid €18 for an average sandwich today. This is why I'm building Cheap Eats.").
  - Day 3: A behind-the-scenes detail (data sources, ranking logic, design choices).
- Each post ends with: *"Cheap Eats launches soon — get notified at everydaystudio.com."*

**Week 3 — Beta hunt**
- Open a small **closed beta** for Cheap Eats. Ask for 20-50 testers.
- Recruit from: your newsletter, X followers, friends, local Reddit (r/<your-city>).
- Real beta testers = real testimonials at launch.

**Week 4 — Pre-launch push**
- Publish a "Coming next Tuesday: Cheap Eats" post everywhere.
- DM 20 people from related niches (food bloggers, frugal communities, local creators) and ask if they'd be willing to try it day-of.
- Schedule the Product Hunt launch.

## 4. Cheap Eats launch day (one Tuesday)

### The launch stack (do all of these on the same day)

| Channel | What to post |
|---|---|
| **Product Hunt** | Schedule for 12:01 AM PT. Have a 60-sec demo video. Reply to every comment within 1 hour. |
| **Newsletter** | Send to your full list. Subject: *"Cheap Eats is live."* One short story, one big button. |
| **X/Twitter** | A short launch thread. Pin it. Tag relevant niche accounts (frugal, food, local). |
| **Reddit** | Post in r/SideProject, r/InternetIsBeautiful, r/Frugal, and **your local city sub** (the most important one). Lead with the problem, not the app. |
| **Indie Hackers** | "I just launched my first app" post with your full studio story. |
| **Hacker News** | "Show HN: Cheap Eats — a curated list of genuinely good cheap food near you (by an indie studio)." |
| **Local press** | Email 3 local food bloggers / city blogs / journalists. Pitch the **angle**, not the app: *"A local indie maker built an app to expose real prices at <city> restaurants."* |

### Targets for launch day
- Top 5 on Product Hunt for the day
- 500+ Cheap Eats installs
- 500+ newsletter total subscribers
- 1 piece of local press coverage

## 5. Post-launch: turn launch attention into a real studio

You'll have a 1-week burst of attention. Convert it into permanence:

- **In-app prompt:** "Like Cheap Eats? Get notified when Everyday Studio ships its next app." → newsletter signup.
- **Email autoresponder:** Every new subscriber gets a 3-email welcome series: (1) the studio's story, (2) what's coming next, (3) a request — "What everyday problem should we solve next?"
- **Survey your first 100 users:** What do they want next? Don't guess the next app — let them tell you.
- **Pick app #2 publicly.** Announce the choice on X/newsletter: *"You voted. The next Everyday Studio app is…"* That post itself becomes free marketing.

---

## 6. The "Studio flywheel" — how scaling will work later

Once you have **2+ shipped apps**, cross-pollination kicks in:

1. **"More from Everyday Studio" screen** in every app, linking siblings.
2. **One newsletter for all apps.** Cheap Eats users hear when app #2 launches.
3. **One brand voice on social.** You post for Everyday Studio, not individual apps.
4. **Shared design system.** Each new app ships faster.
5. **Public roadmap.** Users vote on the next app — free market research + pre-launch demand.

**The math:** if each app sends 10% of its users to the next, and you ship 2 apps a year, by year 3 your launches start with a baseline audience of thousands — for free. But this only works **once you have more than one app**, so focus on Cheap Eats first.

## 7. ASO for Cheap Eats (biggest free lever)

### App Store title (30 chars)
`Cheap Eats: Good food, low cost`

### Subtitle / Short description
*"Find genuinely good food near you that won't wreck your wallet."*

### Description (first 3 lines matter most)
1. **The problem:** "You're hungry, you want something good, you don't want to overpay."
2. **What it does:** "Cheap Eats shows you real prices at real places near you, curated and ranked honestly."
3. **3-5 bullets of features.**
4. **Why it's different:** No fake reviews. No sponsored ranking. No tracking.
5. **About Everyday Studio** (paste in every future app too):

> Everyday Studio is a small, independent studio building useful apps for daily life. No tracking, no dark patterns, no nonsense. Learn more at everydaystudio.com.

### Screenshots (the real conversion driver)
- First screenshot = **headline + benefit**, not a UI shot.
- Show real, recognizable scenarios (a plate of food + a real price).
- Use the same template for every future app so the studio feels cohesive.

### Keywords
Use **AppFollow** or **Sensor Tower free tier** to find low-competition keywords:
- "cheap eats near me"
- "cheap food"
- "budget restaurants <city>"

## 8. 90-day plan

### Days 1–14 — Studio foundation
- Buy domain, deploy site, set up email + analytics + handles.
- Publish the studio launch story everywhere.
- Open Cheap Eats closed beta to 20-50 testers.

### Days 15–45 — Build in public
- 3 posts/week on X about building Cheap Eats.
- 1 post/week on Indie Hackers.
- Grow newsletter to 200.
- Polish Cheap Eats based on beta feedback.

### Days 46–60 — Launch Cheap Eats
- Full launch stack (Product Hunt, Reddit, HN, newsletter, local press).
- Daily reply-to-everything mode for 1 week.

### Days 61–90 — Convert & decide app #2
- Survey users.
- Publish "next app" announcement based on votes.
- Start building in public for app #2.
- Grow newsletter to 1,000.

## 10. Things to NOT do (common indie traps)

- ❌ Don't market 5 apps when you have 1. You'll look amateur.
- ❌ Don't launch Cheap Eats without an audience. Spend 4 weeks building one first.
- ❌ Don't run paid ads before ASO is dialed in.
- ❌ Don't post on every social network. Pick **X** (best for indie/maker audience) and **one of TikTok/Reels** (best for consumer reach). Own those two.
- ❌ Don't add subscriptions to apps that don't need them. Your brand promise is "no nonsense" — protect it.
- ❌ Don't write generic launch posts. Always lead with the **specific moment** the app solves.
- ❌ Don't reveal app #2 until you've actually started building it. Hype dies fast without follow-through.
